1. Grind Fresh

For the best flavor, grind your coffee beans just before brewing. Different brewing methods require different grind sizes, so experiment with fine, medium, and coarse grinds.

2. Water Temperature

The ideal temperature for brewing coffee is between 90-96°C (195-205°F). Too hot, and it can make coffee bitter; too cool, and it might taste under-extracted.

3. Bloom the Ground

When using a French press or pour-over, wet the grounds with a small amount of hot water and let them sit for 30 seconds before adding the rest. This “blooming” releases CO₂ and enhances flavor.

4. Use Filtered Water

Tap water can add off-flavors to coffee, so if possible, use filtered water for a purer taste.

5. Experiment with Ratios

Start with a 1:15 coffee-to-water ratio (1 gram of coffee per 15 grams of water), then adjust based on taste preference.
